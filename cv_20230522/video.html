Unity & C# Shenanigans

The idea of making video games always tickled my fancy and is one of the main reasons why I even started programming. When I heard about Unity Engine I had to give it a try. A test run of my crude level is in the video. The song in the background is BFG Division by Mick Gordon. Seemed appropriate.

I've used ray tracing for shooting mechanics, classic AWSD & mouse-look FPS movement setup. Shots at non-destructible objects result in green spheres on hit locations. Yellow cubes are destructible – they shatter under impulse. Red ones go "boom". The crosshair is just a single asterisk character, barely visible in the center. At the time, I was more focused on the code and functionality rather than the looks. All 3D models and animations were done by me – I learned some Blender modeling previously, which proved very useful on multiple occasions, especially for correcting complex geometry prior to 3D printing.
