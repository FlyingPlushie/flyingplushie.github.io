Buggy & Artificial Neural Networks

This project is one of my favorites – it got me into artificial intelligence. The idea was building the cheapest possible mobile robot and making it move through unstructured 2D space without collisions using artificial neural networks – ANNs.

Three infrared rangefinders proved sufficient when each was mounted facing forward, left and right, away from the robot – buggy. I made a separate 4-button controller to navigate the buggy through specific obstacles and areas while recording training data from the sensors. Variety of neural networks were trained in MATLAB, their parameters properly formatted and used on the robot's 32-bit ARM microcontroller, programmed in mikroC. Roughly 60 variants of trained networks were tested on the robot, but only 5 displayed good performance while moving the robot through unknown 2D space.

Along the way, I learned how to think when applying neural nets, what to look for, what to be wary of and I've finally seen the neural nets as they truly are – big parameter matrices, obeying the math like everything else. Previously, it was just magic that got mentioned when you run out of analytical solutions.
