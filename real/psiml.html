PSIML6 & AI Stretch

PSIML6 - Petnica Summer Institute of Machine Learning, the 6th. It started as a call to students that I stumbled upon shortly after finishing Professor Ng's Machine Learning Course back in 2020. After passing the competitive stage that pushed me harder than I could've anticipated, I got accepted into summer school. Before the lectures I may have had some illusions of knowing something, a bit of an ego-trip. But, when the lectures started I was quickly humbled and reminded how vast was the universe I tried to grasp. Our lecturers, people from the Faculty of Mathematics, School of Electrical Engineering, Microsoft Development Center in Serbia and from the Industry were exquisite – the experience was a privilege. The only downside was the event's online format – epidemic made the real trip impossible.

I learned so much in so little time, it was phenomenal. I selected gesture recognition as my project, thinking it would prove useful later, during my PhD studies. The results are in this GitHub repo. We used PyTorch. It's nothing spectacular, I finished late, but it works and it taught me a ton – which was the point.

Experiences from PSIML6 shaped some of my PhD work. Shortly after the seminar I got my hands on the RGB-D camera and started tinkering with a real-time transfer of movements from a human subject to an industrial robot. That is still a work in progress.
