My Non-Linear Development Path

Until Master's Degree

I've been programming since my third year of high school. Started with Pascal and the thrill that came from seeing the computer do exactly as I tell it to, whether right or wrong. Continued with MATLAB while studying mechanical engineering. Professor of kinematics pointed me towards freeBSD and I clicked with Linux, command line and vim, then distro-hopped until settled with Ubuntu. Picked up touch-typing along the way – my current speed is around 100 WPM. Learned some C for my Master's Thesis that focused on the variants of linear and circular interpolations for real-time control of a plasma cutter via microcontroller.

PhD Studies in Mechanical Engineering

During my PhD studies, some projects were overwhelming, shoved me neck deep. But, I persevered – it was too much fun to quit. It started along the lines of "You know some C? Great, now you'll learn C++ and use it together with OpenGL." Afterwards, I just kept pushing. Learned Python on a hunch, seemed cool enough. Indeed, it supercharged my selftaught learning pace and following several projects, I had a solid grasp of the OOP, git and a few good programming practices.

Beyond

Since I found LeetCode and got into algorithms and data structures, I've become a regular and still work consistently to keep myself sharp. Also, I took up learning Java since Sedgewick's algorithms book is written in it, making it easier to follow through.

Currently, I don't know what my next project will be, but I'm sure it's gonna be awesome!

Programming Language Omnivore

C/C++ and Python are my main tools. Java is the most recent, but it grows on me. Played with Lisp/Scheme through beautiful SICP. A touch of C# for Unity games. A smidgen of JavaScript, CSS & HTML because bland presentations lose your audience. And LaTeX for serious texts.

Jack of All Trades

"Read without mercy" my professor said and I take that quite seriously – still mind rots. New language? Great. New tech? Lemme see. Better framework? I'm in. Smarter way to do something? Yes. My craft is paramount to me and the only way to hone it is through surpassing my current limits.

Technician

Solution must serve well. I always strived to create things that people loved using, finding a human side within the machine. I admire and aspire to be like the ones whose solutions stand the test of time.

Paladin at Heart

I play fair, by the rules and carry myself with my team, appreciating the people around me, even when the going gets tough. It's not the end of the world, just an obstacle, so we'll find a way through. Or around.
